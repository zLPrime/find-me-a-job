# Skill: Application Writing

## Purpose

Produce the remaining pieces of an application package — cover letter,
answers to application-specific questions, submission notes — grounded
strictly in the candidate profile and the specific vacancy, assembled
into an [application package](../templates/application.md) for candidate
review.

## When to Invoke

- After a tailored CV exists for a matched vacancy and the candidate
  wants to move toward submitting an application.

## Required Inputs

- The candidate profile.
- The vacancy artifact, including any application-specific questions or
  required formats.
- The tailored CV produced for this vacancy.
- The match decision log entry.

## Expected Outputs

- A complete application package draft: cover letter, question responses,
  and submission notes, all marked as draft pending review.

## Quality Criteria

- Cover letter and answers reference only facts present in the candidate
  profile; no invented anecdotes, numbers, or claims.
- Responses directly address what the employer actually asked, rather
  than generic boilerplate.
- Tone is professional and specific to this employer and role, not a
  form letter with the company name swapped in.
- Every open question needed from the candidate before submission
  (missing info, ambiguous prompts) is listed explicitly.
- The package is clearly marked as requiring candidate approval before
  anything is sent, per [rules/general.md](../rules/general.md).

## Limitations

- This skill produces drafts only. It never submits an application or
  sends a message on the candidate's behalf.
- It cannot answer application questions that require information the
  candidate hasn't provided — those become open questions, not
  best-guess answers.

## Future Improvements

- Build a small library of question archetypes (e.g., "why this
  company," "describe a challenge") with guidance on grounding each in
  the candidate profile without becoming formulaic.
- Add guidance for multi-stage application processes (e.g., follow-up
  questions after an initial submission).
